About Me

Currently I work as a domain-specific language engineer at itemis. My work mainly focusses on developing customer-specific languages based on domain-specific requirements, usually involving model-driven design patterns. We heavily use the JetBrains MPS language engineering tool. At itemis, part of my work also involves active contribution to our open-source language tooling: the IETS3.opensource language collection, and the mbeddr platform.

Before my work at itemis, I did my PhD at MPI-SWS in the group for Automated Verification and Approximation lead by Eva Darulova. During my PhD I have worked on verified compilation and optimization of floating-point programs, and the ergonomics of interactive theorem provers. This website is primarily focused on the research work I have conducted. For my technical contributions, please also see my GitHub profile.
